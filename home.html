Hồ Chí Minh

The revolutionary who led a rebellion that overthrew colonial rule

A quick summary of the life of Hồ Chí Minh

  • 1890 - Hồ Chí Minh was born as Nguyễn Sinh Cung in the village of Hoàng Trù, his mother's village.
  • 1908 - He received a French education, attending Collège Quốc học in Huế.
  • 1912 - While working as the cook's helper on a ship, Hồ traveled to the United States. He worked a series of menial jobs until around 1918.
  • 1919 - While living in France, he joined the Groupe des Patriotes Annamites (The Group of Vietnamese Patriots). They had been publishing newspaper articles advocating for Vietnamese independence from French colonial rule.
  • 1923 - Ho left Paris for Moscow carrying a passport with the name Chen Vang, a Chinese merchant, where he was employed by the Comintern, studied at the Communist University of the Toilers of the East, and participated in the Fifth Comintern Congress in June 1924.
  • 1938 - Moved to China and served as an advisor to the Chinese Communist armed forces.
  • 1940 - He began regularly using the name Hồ Chí Minh, a Vietnamese name combining a common Vietnamese surname (Hồ, 胡) with a given name meaning "He Who has been enlightened" (from Sino-Vietnamese 志 明: Chí meaning "will" or "spirit" and Minh meaning "bright").
  • 1941 - Hồ Chí Minh returned to Vietnam to lead the Việt Minh independence movement.
  • 1945 - Following the August Revolution organized by the Việt Minh, Hồ Chí Minh became Chairman of the Provisional Government (Premier of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam) and issued a Proclamation of Independence of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam. Although he convinced Emperor Bảo Đại to abdicate, his government was not recognized by any country. He repeatedly petitioned President Harry S. Truman for support for Vietnamese independence, citing the Atlantic Charter, but Truman never responded.
